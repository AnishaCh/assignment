Internet Search

Using the world wide web, without knowing how to navigate it raised an issue. Initially, finding the desired web page was challenging. There were multiple websites which led to page after page to locate the desired web page, this led to the need for web search. Initially there were a set of web pages listed and people had to search through it. Yahoo headed to become the founders of internet search. It also proved that it is possible to make money on the web , especially through advertisements. Another search startup which aimed to focus on developing a search engine is Excite. The technology that excite developed was more sophisticated than that of yahoo.Yahoo had a list of sites sorted by humans but excite was completely software based. When we type in a query the software looks for the desired pages based on the terms entered. The search initially was undesirable leading to unwanted pages based on the key words. This was a trigger for google. Google overcame this with a solution. The websites we are looking for are shown based on the counts of links i.e how many pages point to the page you are looking for. Google gave a tough competition to Yahoo and Excite. The search engine proved to be one of the world's best market search and analysis platforms. Google had a large user base, but no revenue-generating business model. Doerr conducted study into how to develop a business plan for it. Larry and Sergey did not want to include advertisements because they wanted users to enjoy using this search engine. They obtained their idea by stealing it from Bill Gross (founder, IdeaLab). They planned to sell keywords to marketers because they believed that advertisers would be prepared to pay a lot of money for keyword information. Bill Gross was scheduled to meet with Google. Google launched AdWords, a service that was comparable to Overture (the company with keyword ideas given by Bill Gross). Gross sued Google for this, but the case was resolved for a large sum of money.
